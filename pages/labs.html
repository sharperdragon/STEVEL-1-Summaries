Test Purpose Notes
Troponin I/T Myocardial infarction diagnosis Elevated within hours; peaks at 24–48h; specific for cardiac injury
Alkaline Phosphatase (ALP) Bone and hepatobiliary pathology ↑ in bone growth, liver obstruction; check GGT for hepatic specificity
LDH (Lactate Dehydrogenase) Marker of cell turnover or injury Elevated in hemolysis, MI, cancers, pneumocystis pneumonia
INR / PT / PTT Coagulation pathway evaluation INR/PT = extrinsic (VII); PTT = intrinsic (VIII, IX, XI, XII); both ↑ in DIC
CA-125 Ovarian cancer marker Non-specific
High in endometriosis, menstruation, peritonitis
AFP (Alpha-Fetoprotein) Hepatocellular carcinoma, yolk sac tumor, NTDs ↑ in HCC, ↓ in Down syndrome
also elevated in twin pregnancy
CEA (Carcinoembryonic Antigen) Colon cancer marker Low specificity
used to monitor recurrence not for screening
CA 19-9 Pancreatic adenocarcinoma marker Elevated in biliary disease too
low sensitivity in early disease
GGT (Gamma-glutamyl transferase) Hepatic enzyme ↑ in alcohol use and cholestasis
distinguishes hepatic ALP from bone
Haptoglobin Marker of intravascular hemolysis Low in intravascular hemolysis
normal in extravascular
Reticulocyte Count Bone marrow response to anemia ↑ in hemolysis or blood loss
↓ in aplastic anemia or marrow failure
Schilling Test Evaluates B12 absorption Used to distinguish dietary deficiency vs. pernicious anemia vs. malabsorption
Fractional Excretion of Sodium (FENa) Differentiates causes of AKI <1% suggests prerenal
>2% suggests intrinsic renal damage
Urine Anion Gap Assesses cause of non-anion gap acidosis Positive in renal tubular acidosis
negative in GI bicarb loss
Urinary Cyanide-Nitroprusside Test Screens for cystinuria Turns purple with cystine
confirms aminoaciduria
Urine osmolality vs. serum osmolality Evaluates causes of hyponatremia Helps distinguish SIADH (↑ urine osm) vs. DI (↓ urine osm)
Urine chloride Differentiates metabolic alkalosis causes Low in vomiting; high in Bartter/Gitelman or diuretic use
CSF PCR for HSV Detects HSV encephalitis Most sensitive and specific test; rapid diagnosis
Quantiferon-TB Gold / IGRA Latent tuberculosis screening More specific than PPD; not affected by BCG vaccine
NAAT (gonorrhea/chlamydia) STI diagnosis Gold standard; detects bacterial DNA/RNA
Dark-field microscopy Treponema pallidum detection Used for primary syphilis; visualizes spirochetes
PAS stain of intestinal biopsy Diagnoses Whipple disease Stains Tropheryma whipplei-laden macrophages
AFB (Acid-Fast Bacilli) stain Tuberculosis and Nocardia detection Ziehl-Neelsen stain highlights acid-fast organisms
CSF 14-3-3 protein Marker for prion disease Elevated in Creutzfeldt-Jakob disease
EEG: triphasic waves Findings in hepatic encephalopathy Correlates with metabolic encephalopathy
CSF albuminocytologic dissociation Guillain-Barré syndrome ↑ protein with normal WBC count in CSF
Edrophonium (Tensilon) Test Myasthenia gravis diagnosis (historical) Transient symptom improvement with AChE inhibitor
Plasma Metanephrines Pheochromocytoma screening High sensitivity; confirmatory test after symptoms
Water Deprivation Test Diabetes insipidus vs. psychogenic polydipsia In central DI, desmopressin increases urine osm
Ortolani / Barlow maneuvers Congenital hip dysplasia screening Physical exam clicks/clunks with hip instability
Sweat Chloride Test Diagnoses cystic fibrosis ↑ sweat chloride due to CFTR dysfunction
Ames Test Mutagenicity screening (lab concept) Detects DNA mutagens using bacteria; Step 1 buzzword
Carboxyhemoglobin level Carbon monoxide poisoning Elevated in CO exposure; falsely normal pulse ox
Urinary arsenic, lead, mercury levels Heavy metal toxicity screening Measured via urine; confirms exposure
D-Xylose Absorption Test Malabsorption workup Abnormal in celiac; normal in pancreatic insufficiency
Sudan Stain Screening for fat malabsorption Positive in steatorrhea (e.g. pancreatic insufficiency, celiac)
5-HIAA (5-hydroxyindoleacetic acid) Serotonin metabolite; carcinoid tumor marker Elevated in urine in carcinoid syndrome; avoid serotonin-rich foods before test
C1 Esterase Inhibitor Level Hereditary angioedema diagnosis Low levels or function confirm diagnosis; bradykinin-mediated swelling
VMA (Vanillylmandelic Acid) Metabolite of catecholamines Elevated in pheochromocytoma or neuroblastoma
Heterophile Antibody Test (Monospot) Diagnosis of EBV mononucleosis False negatives in early disease or young children; rapid bedside test
VDRL (Venereal Disease Research Laboratory) Syphilis screening Non-treponemal; false positives (e.g. SLE, pregnancy); confirm with FTA-ABS
Direct Coombs Test Detects antibodies/complement on RBCs Positive in autoimmune hemolysis, hemolytic disease of newborn
Indirect Coombs Test Detects anti-RBC antibodies Prenatal testing
blood transfusion compatibility
